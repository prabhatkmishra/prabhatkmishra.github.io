    Predictive Stochastic Adaptive Learning Machines

    PSALM (Predictive Stochastic Adaptive Learning Machines) Lab at IIT Kharagpur is focused on research at the intersection of control theory and Artificial Intelligence (AI). The real world applications of AI require safety guarantee and explainability of the AI module. In addition, several modules such as sensing and perception, planning and action need to interact to complete the feedback loop. Our main focus is in planning and decision making but we also consider sensing and actuation along with the effects of communication channels whenever required.

    About me

    I am an Assistant Professor with the Centre of Excellence in Artificial Intelligence (CoEAI), Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur (IITKgp), India, and currently working at the intersection of control and learning. I am developing algorithms for autonomous systems to make them smarter with the help of data and distribution. I use control theory to provide safety certificates, and to enhance explainability and trustworthiness of AI driven techniques.

    I did PhD from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB), India, on Stochastic Model Predictive Control (MPC) for Cyber Physical Systems. During my PhD, I received a Swiss Government Excellence Scholarship to study at the Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland, for one year. I learnt Gaussian process regression and implemented stochastic MPC on a quadcopter during my stay at EPFL.

    Before joining IITKgp, I was a Postdoctoral Associate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), USA, and working on polynomial chaos theory for advance precision manufacturing applications. Prior to that, I was a Postdoctoral Research Asssociate at the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign (UIUC), USA, where we developed a couple of deep learning supported MPC algorithms and a constrained estimator.
